V.
Common Objections
Q & A record
Q.
Don't these cameras only catch criminals?
A.
99% of the plates scanned belong to people suspected of nothing. The cameras photograph every car, store the data, and check it against hotlists — in that order. You're in the database whether you've done anything wrong or not.
Q.
If you have nothing to hide, why worry?
A.
The question assumes the government gets to decide what counts as suspicious. Location data builds a picture of your life — where you worship, who you see, what you do. That picture shouldn't be stored for three years without your knowledge or a warrant.
Q.
Doesn't this reduce crime?
A.
The evidence is thin. Studies of Flock camera deployments show mixed results — some localized reductions in vehicle theft, little effect on violent crime. What's clear is that the privacy cost is borne by everyone while the claimed benefits are marginal and unevenly distributed.
