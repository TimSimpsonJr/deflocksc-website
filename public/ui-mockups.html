The surveillance pipeline

Every time you drive past an ALPR camera, your license plate number, timestamp, and GPS coordinates are recorded. This happens whether you've done anything wrong or not.

That data flows into the SLED database — accessible to over 430 agencies across South Carolina. There are no state laws governing how long it's kept, or who can access it.

The cameras don't just sit at checkpoints. They're mounted on patrol cars, fixed to bridges, and installed at intersections. Your movements are being mapped continuously.

SC Senate Bill S447 would establish a 30-day retention limit and require judicial oversight for database queries. It's been in committee for two sessions.
